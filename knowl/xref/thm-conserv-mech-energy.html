Theorem 16.3.1.

The change in mechancial energy over an arbitrary time period is equal to the nonconservative work done on the system over that period. In equation form
\begin{equation*} \Delta E_{mech} = W^{nc} \end{equation*}
Where \(W^{nc}\) is the nonconservative work done on the system over the time period in question.
🔗
🔗

Proof.

By the work-kinetic energy theorem Theorem 15.2.1
\begin{equation*} W = \Delta K \end{equation*}
where \(W\) is the total work done on the system over the time period in question. We can split the total work into its conservative and non conservative components.
\begin{equation*} W^{c} + W^{nc} = \Delta K \end{equation*}
By Theorem 16.2.1
\begin{equation*} \Delta U = -W^{c} \end{equation*}
so
\begin{align*} -\Delta U + W^{nc} \amp = \Delta K\\ \\ W^{nc} \amp = \Delta K + \Delta U\\ \\ \Delta E_{mech} \amp = W^{nc} \end{align*}
