Theorem 4.1.3.

When the x-component of the acceleration is constant
\begin{equation*} x_{f} = -\frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + v_{x, f} \Delta t + x_{i}. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.

We rearrange Theorem 4.1.1 to obtain
\begin{equation*} v_{x, i} = v_{x, f} - a_{x} \Delta t. \end{equation*}
Plugging this into Theorem 4.1.2 gives
\begin{align*} x_{f} \amp = \frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + v_{x, i} \Delta t + x_{i} \\ \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + \left( v_{x, f} - a_{x} \Delta t \right) \Delta t + x_{i} \\ \\ \amp = -\frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + v_{x, f} \Delta t + x_{i} \end{align*}
