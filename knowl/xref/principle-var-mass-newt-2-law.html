Principle 17.6.2. Newton’s Second Law for Variable Mass Systems.

Let \(v_{rel}\) denote the relative velocity of the mass being transfered between the system and the environment. If we take the interpretation that mass being transfered between the system and the environment causes an external force
\begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{mass} = \vec{v}_{rel} \frac{dm}{dt} \end{equation*}
on the system, then Newton’s Second Law in the form
\begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{net} = m \vec{a} \end{equation*}
applies to variable mass systems. \(\vec{F}_{mass}\) must be included in \(\vec{F}_{net}\) for this to be true. If multiple simultaneous mass transfers are occuring between the system and the environment, then each mass transfer will exert its own variable mass force on the system.
