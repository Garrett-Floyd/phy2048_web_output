Theorem 17.3.1.

Assume an isolated system of two objects undergo a one dimensional elastic collision and don’t exchange mass with each other. Let \(m_{1}\text{,}\) \(v_{1, i}\text{,}\) and \(v_{1, f}\) be the mass, initial speed, and final speed of object one. Likewise let \(m_{2}\text{,}\) \(v_{2, i}\text{,}\) and \(v_{2, f}\) be the mass, initial speed, and final speed of object two. Then
\begin{equation*} v_{1, f} = \frac{m_{1} - m_{2}}{m_{1} + m_{2}} v_{1, i} + \frac{2m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} v_{2, i}. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.

The kinetic energy conservation equation can be written as
\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2}m_{1}v_{1, i}^{2} + \frac{1}{2}m_{2}v_{2, i}^{2} \amp = \frac{1}{2}m_{1}v_{1, f}^{2} + \frac{1}{2}m_{2}v_{2, f}^{2}\\ \\ m_{1}v_{1, i}^{2} + m_{2}v_{2, i}^{2} \amp = m_{1}v_{1, f}^{2} + m_{2}v_{2, f}^{2}. \end{align*}
Now we put all the terms for object 1 on one side and all the terms for object 2 on the other.
\begin{equation*} m_{1}v_{1, i}^{2} - m_{1}v_{1, f}^{2} = m_{2}v_{2, f}^{2} - m_{2}v_{2, i}^{2}. \end{equation*}
Factoring both sides gives
\begin{equation*} m_{1}(v_{1, i} - v_{1,f})(v_{1, i} + v_{1, f}) = m_{2}(v_{2, f} - v_{2, i})(v_{2, i} + v_{2, f}). \end{equation*}
Doing the same for the conservation of momentum equation gives
\begin{align*} m_{1}v_{1, i} + m_{2}v_{2, i} \amp = m_{1}v_{1, f} + m_{2}v_{2, f} \\ \\ m_{1}(v_{1, i} - v_{1, f}) \amp = m_{2}(v_{2, f} - v_{2, i}). \end{align*}
Dividing our final expression from the conservation of kinetic energy by our final expression from the conservation of momentum gives
\begin{align*} \frac{m_{1}(v_{1, i} - v_{1,f})(v_{1, i} + v_{1, f})}{m_{1}(v_{1, i} - v_{1, f})} \amp = \frac{m_{2}(v_{2, f} - v_{2, i})(v_{2, i} + v_{2, f})}{m_{2}(v_{2, f} - v_{2, i})} \\ \\ v_{1, i} + v_{1, f} \amp = v_{2, i} + v_{2, f}\\ \\ v_{1, f} \amp = v_{2, i} + v_{2, f} - v_{1, i}. \end{align*}
Now we plugg this back in to the origional conservation of momentum equation
\begin{align*} m_{1}v_{1, i} + m_2v_{2, i} \amp = m_{1}v_{1, f} + m_2(v_{1, i} + v_{1, f} - v_{2, i}) \\ \\ (m_{1}- m_{2})v_{1, i} + 2m_2v_{2, i} \amp = (m_{1} + m_{2})v_{1, f} \\ \\ v_{1, f} \amp = (\frac{m_{1}- m_{2}}{m_{1} + m_{2}})v_{1, i} + (\frac{2m_2}{m_{1} + m_{2}})v_{2, i}. \end{align*}
