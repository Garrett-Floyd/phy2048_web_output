Principle 10.1.2. Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.

Imagine there are two particles, one having mass \(m\) and the other having mass \(m^{\prime}\text{.}\) Let \(\vec{R}\) be the relative postion of \(m\) with respect to \(m^{\prime}\text{.}\) The gravitational force \(\vec{F}_{g}\) on the particle of mass \(m\) due to the particle of mass \(m^{\prime}\) is
\begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{g} = -G \frac{m m^{\prime}}{R^{2}} \hat{R} \end{equation*}
where \(G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \frac{m^{3}}{kg \cdot {sec}^2}\text{.}\)
