Theorem 4.1.5.

When the x-component of the acceleration is constant
\begin{equation*} v_{x, f}^{2} = v_{x, i}^{2} + 2a_{x} \left(x_{f} - x_{i} \right). \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.

We rearrange Theorem 4.1.1 to obtain
\begin{equation*} \Delta t = \frac{v_{x, f} - v_{x, i}}{a_x}. \end{equation*}
Plugging this into Theorem 4.1.2 gives
\begin{align*} x_{f} \amp = \frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + v_{x, i} \Delta t + x_{i} \\ \\ \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = \frac{1}{2} a_{x} \Delta t^{2} + v_{x, i} \Delta t \\ \\ \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = \frac{1}{2} a_{x} \left( \frac{v_{x, f} - v_{x, i}}{a_{x}} \right)^{2} + v_{x, i} \left( \frac{v_{x, f} - v_{x, i}}{a_x} \right) \\ \\ \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = \frac{1}{2a_{x}}\left( v_{x, f} - v_{x, i} \right)^{2} + v_{x, i} \left( \frac{v_{x, f} - v_{x, i}}{a_x} \right) \\ \\ 2 a_{x} \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = \left( v_{x, f} - v_{x, i} \right)^{2} + 2 v_{x, i} \left( v_{x, f} - v_{x, i} \right) \\ \\ 2 a_{x} \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = v_{x, f}^{2} - 2 v_{x, f} v_{x, i} + v_{x, i}^{2} + 2 v_{x, i} v_{x, f} - 2 v_{x, i}^{2}\\ \\ 2 a_{x} \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \amp = v_{x, f}^{2} - v_{x, i}^{2}\\ \\ v_{x, f}^{2} \amp = v_{x, i}^{2} + 2 a_{x} \left( x_{f} - x_{i} \right) \end{align*}
